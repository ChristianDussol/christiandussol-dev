# How I learn

Certifications are forcing functions, not credentials. Nobody has ever hired me because of a badge. But every certification forced me to sit with a domain long enough to stop guessing, and what fell out of that process is on this site.

### The method

I learn by writing. Not as a note-taking habit, but as a test: if I cannot explain a trade-off in writing, I have not understood it. Scott Young's UltraLearning gave that instinct a structure, and five of its principles now shape how I approach any new domain.

**Directness.** Real projects from day one. No toy examples, because a hello-world deployment teaches you nothing about what breaks at 3 AM.

**Drilling.** Isolate the weak point and attack it specifically, rather than re-reading the parts you already know because they feel comfortable.

**Feedback.** Deploy it, monitor it, watch it break, fix it. The cluster is a harsher reviewer than any exam.

**Retrieval.** Practice without the documentation open. Recognising an answer is not the same as producing one.

**Building over consuming.** Deploy, test, break, learn, repeat. Reading about a technology and running it in production are separated by everything that matters.
